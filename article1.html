WHAT IS COUNTER STRIKE GLOBAL OFFENSIVE?

Counter-Strike (CS) is a series of multiplayer tactical first-person shooter video games in which teams of terrorists battle to perpetrate an act of terror (bombing, hostage-taking, assassination) while counter-terrorists try to prevent it (bomb defusal, hostage rescue, escort mission). The series began on Windows in 1999 with the release of the first game, Counter-Strike. It was initially released as a modification ("mod") for Half-Life that was designed by Minh "Gooseman" Le and Jess "Cliffe" Cliffe before the rights to the mod's intellectual property were acquired by Valve, the developers of Half-Life, who then turned Counter-Strike into a retail product released in 2000.

Why Counter Strike Is Still Succeeding In Esports?

In the electronic sports industry, where everything happens with tremendous speed, it is surprising to see actors, whether they are players, competition organizers or disciplines that last over time. That is precisely the curious case of Counter-Strike, a game that, in its different versions, has been one of the flagships of esports since 1999.

This shooter, which began its journey in the video game ecosystem as an addition, a modification of the historic Half-Life, soon acquired its own share of prominence to be released individually. Such was the irruption of Counter-Strike in the embryonic esports scene that with just a year of life, in August 2000 it was already the main attraction of a competitive circuit that It ended up becoming an icon of electronic sports in the first decade of the 21st century, the CPL.

The golden era of Counter-Strike in esports

The growth, both in the mass of players and in the follow-up of the tournaments, was exponential and constant throughout its first decade of life, counting as its peak of maximum splendor around the years 2007 and 2008, a moment when great players in the current ecosystem, like ESL, with their Intel Extreme Masters they showcased the Counter-Strike spectacle all over the world with major competitions.

While its "modern" version, Counter-Strike: Source, grew and discussed the acquisition of new members for its community in parallel, nothing seemed to shake the foundations of the reign of the original. However, the years passed and tedium and an already outdated graphics engine made it decline in relevance within the world of esports at the same time that new titles originally focused on that sector appeared.

Its worst moment coincided with the beginning of the development of the current version, Counter-Strike: Global Offensive, designed to recover the throne and which perhaps came a little late. Around the year 2010, the following of the competitions and the user base decreased dramatically and it was not until 2012 that the now acclaimed CSGO was launched.

The beginnings were not easy, the market share in Counter-Strike esports was low, which implied a development of the competitive ecosystem much slower than desired. Something that ESL and Dreamhack, the main exponents of the organization of competitions years ago, saw as an opportunity to continue being leaders in the sector. The bet did not cease at any time and proof of this is that today they make up the most prestigious competitive circuit on the calendar.
