WHAT IS VALORANT?

It is a 5v5 multiplayer first person shooter (FPS) where one team attacks and the other defends. The main game mode, Search and Destroy, is very similar to CS:GO. The attacking team's goal is to plant a bomb (called a spike) and cause it to detonate, while the defending team tries to avoid it. Regardless of whether to wage war or not, if a squadron is eliminated before any other victory condition is met, the opposing squadron will win.

Matches last for 25 rounds, each lasting 100 seconds. The first team to win 13 rounds wins the match overall. At the start of the round, you will have 30 seconds to purchase weapons and equipment for that round. If you die in a round, you will have to wait until the next one for that round. If you die in a round. You will have to wait until the next round to respawn. This main game mode can be played in ranked or unranked matches.

Valorant: The keys to the game that will revolutionize eSports

The commentator and professional player Ulises Prieto almost speaks of obsession. "I think Valorant has hit the key, it's been years since I've come across a game that I literally go to bed thinking about," says this veteran eSports caster. "While I'm sleeping, my partner has come to tell me that I tap on his leg," he adds.

The new video game from the American company Riot Games, the creators of the acclaimed League of Legends (LoL), can have this effect on many players. It is a tactical first-person shooter similar to the popular and classic Counter Strike, but with different characters and unique powers. It is not quite a MOBA, a type of video game in which the abilities of each champion define the way of playing, like Dota or LoL itself, although it does take some elements of the genre. If you know how to shoot, you'll be fine, but you'd better use all the powers at your disposal.

The players' first contact with Valorant was between Friday, March 27, and Sunday, March 29, in an online event in which La Vanguardia was able to participate along with hundreds of professional players, streamers, and journalists from around the world. In those three days of testing, it became clear that Riot Games goal was to create the next great eSport, a new video game that would force itself into the most competitive scene in electronic sports.

Being in the closed beta phase, as of April 7, Valorant has achieved exceptional results thanks to being a solid product that the public liked and the collaboration between Riot Games and Twitch, the main video game streaming platform. If you wanted to enter this testing phase, you had to watch streams of the game on this Amazon platform. It was not necessary to see many hours, but you know... People tend to sin more by excess than by default.

Since the beginning of April, the numbers have decreased. For several weeks it remained around 700,000 viewers with thousands of streamers around the world spending half the day live. "I don't do 6, 7 or 8 hours live because I want to be live, I do them because I want to spend 8 hours playing," says Prieto.
