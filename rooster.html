Team Pro Players

Mitr0

Dmitri Van de Vrie also known as "Mitr0" is one of the best-known legends and with the largest number of fans and followers of the Fortnite competitive scene, former Team Liquid player and currently retired, he managed to play a very good role in this great game that even after 5 years since its creation it continues to be one of the best video games related to eSports competition.

The young competitive player made more than $800,000 from Fortnite Battle Royale competitions. He ranks second in total career earnings in Fortnite in the Netherlands, right behind Dave "Rojo" Jong.

While Dimitri made a lot of money from the battle royale game, the truth is that he hasn't been very successful lately, which may have been the reason why he decided to quit it. In the last two years, Mitr0 has made only $7,500 from Fortnite tournaments, a far cry from the $77,500 he made in 2022 and almost half a million dollars in 2019.

K1ng

Thiago “K1ng” Lapp was just a 13-year-old boy who spent his days training Fortnite and studying. From August 2018 to May 2019, he was part of the Sinisters Esports roster until he decided to leave for personal reasons. However, he never stopped practicing his eight hours a day in the Epic Games battle royale and getting fully into the tournament's online qualifiers.

At just 13 years old, Thiago Lapp traveled abroad with his father for the first time to make history. Saturday July 27 was their first day, K1ng and xOwN had their big moment in the Fortnite World Cup Duos Final at New York's Arthur Tennis Stadium. Although the youngsters finished in 39th place, they pocketed a prize of $100,000 in total and the great feat of participating in the first battle royale tournament.

On Sunday the 28th the moment of truth arrived, Thiago “K1ng” Lapp faced the 100 best in the world. The 13-year-old, with an aggressive and proactive style of play, managed to place fifth in the Fortnite World Cap and won a prize of $900,000, bringing a total of $973,000 and marking a milestone in history. of electronic sports in our country.

Mongraal

Kyle Jackson (born: August 13, 2004, better known online as Mongraal, is an English eSports player who mainly plays the game Fortnite. At 13 years old, he had found success, and was featured on BBC News. Kyle was a player for Team Secret (joined in April 2018), but decided to leave the clan on July 7, 2019 to join FaZe Clan.

Summer Skirmish is a Weekly tournament hosted by Fortnite themselves which comes in on Multiple days. Kyle was not allowed to join due to him being too young, as Kyle did mention that he is one of the top players when it came to other tournaments. Kyle also mentioned that he cannot control his age and is unfair as he is the youngest competitive player in the world.

In April, 2018, Kyle was interviewed by the company 'BBC' (British Broadcasting Corporation) as he was the #1 British Fortnite Player and Youngest Competitive Fortnite player. Kyle had mentioned that he had played games his whole life, and started playing more professionally at the age of 9, when he was playing Halo and Call of Duty. There were also questions about Kyle's school work. Kyle responded saying that he has time to study, and is doing well at all subjects in school, and his parents don't have any worry in him playing a lot.

MrSavage

Martin Foss Anderson (born: November 12, 2004, better known online as MrSavage (formerly MrSavageM), is a Norwegian gaming YouTuber known for playing the game, Fortnite: Battle Royale. A chunk of his latest exposure is a video of him getting 40 eliminations in Fortnite, on the first day of Season 9. He is also known for being one of the youngest competitive Fortnite players, alongside collaborating with other young competitive players such as Mongraal, Mitr0 and benjyfishy.
